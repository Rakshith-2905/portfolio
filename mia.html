MIA-Medical Imaging Assistant

About the Project

Artificial Intelligence is changing the world in a very rapid pace. Every buisness and service has been seeing some asspect of automation. The world of healthcare is no exception, it had a steady dose of technological adoption with medical instrumentations, EHR, assistive surgery and many more. Even with all these advancements medical professionals have one of the hardest jobs with need of accuray, prescion and dilligence.

MIA is such an advancement that steps up the game of diagnosis and unbudrens the doctors from staring at medical images by giving diagnostics reports. With the advancements in CNN and the open availability of data medical AI can have a very good accuracy and aid in diagnostics.

Operation

The project analyzes chest X-Rays and classifies if the patient has pneumonia or tuberclosis and draws a heat map indicating the affected areas of the chest. The solution also analyzes bone X-Rays to classify if the bone is broken.

The pneumonia and tuberclosis classification system uses Densenet 121 trained over CheXpert dataset. CheXpert is a large dataset of radiologist-labeled chest X-rays for automated chest x-ray interpretation. The densenet model was developed in pytorch with Imagenet weights and the model was trained on the two labels in a Jetson Tx2.

A heatmap which highlights the spread of the disease was developed by generating class activation maps using global average pooling in the CNN. The models was developed using pytorch framework and the prediction system was docerized and made as an API which accepts image and returns a heatmap and the diagnosis.

For fracture classification the MURA data set was used, MURA (musculoskeletal radiographs) is a large dataset of bone X-rays with labels of fracture. VGG-19 was used for the classificatio. The network was developed and trained using keras. Parelelling the chest X-Ray network this network was also dockerized and made into a API.
