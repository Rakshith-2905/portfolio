Automated Cup Sorting using Computer Vision

About the Project

The hand off counter at coffee shops are very crowded during peek hours. This makes the customers to check all the cups before they get to their cup. This project is to ease the customer taking his cofee cup from the handoff plane. I used vision prosessing techniques to dynamically identify the coffe cups and digitalize the counter to display the name of the drink.

Skills Involved

  • OpenCV
  • Vision Processing in python
  • Camera calibration
  • 3d Design
  • SQl- Databases
  • Markers and Tags in Vision

More About The Project

This process automation starts from the place where the order is taken. The Cashier tags a cup with the order by scaning an unique aruco marker (Learn more here) and storing the data on a SQL table. After this the cup is passed for making the drink, at the Kitchen counter a camera reads the QR code and displays the details of the drink to the Barrista. Once the drink is made the Barrista places the cup on the pick up counter for the customer to pick up.
The Hand off counter has a LCD screen below the counter top for displaying the drink name. A structure was made to mount the camera above. To get the transformations from pixal coordinates of the camera to the pixal coordinates of the screen and finally to the real world position of the cup a homography matrix was calculated using a caliberation cup. The homography matrix is used to transform perspective to fit a circle to be drawn below the cup and the details of the drink is displayed around the circle.
